What Is Code Conquest?

Code Conquest is an original hackathon community, game, and event structure created by KidOYO — New York's first K–12 School Hackathon League for both Middle School and High School divisions.

It challenges young hackers with creative computing problems in team-based events, blending real programming skill with strategic thinking. Teams from districts spanning Eastern Suffolk to Western Nassau compete across Long Island.

KidOYO is also a College Board–approved online provider of curriculum resources for the AP Computer Science Principles exam.

How It Works

🎲

Formatted like the board game Risk — strategy meets code.

01

The Map

Each team receives a digital game map divided into territories. The goal: claim as many territories as possible before time runs out.

02

The Challenges

Each territory holds a coding challenge. Teams must solve it using one of the accepted languages — Python, Java, Web (HTML/CSS/JS), Scratch/Hatch!, or Sprite Editor.

03

Earning Territory

An accurate solve earns that territory for your team and changes its color on the live map. But speed alone isn't everything — quality matters too.

04

Adjacent Only

Teams can only target territories adjacent to ones they already control, just like in Risk. Strategic map positioning is as important as coding ability.

05

The Judges

Judges evaluate all submissions. If a rival team's solution is deemed stronger, they can steal the territory — even if yours was technically correct.

06

The Winner

The team holding the most territories when time expires wins the event and claims the trophy. Come-from-behind victories are very much possible.

About KidOYO

KidOYO is an educational technology platform built around the idea that coding is a thinking skill. Their philosophy is that the structured requirements of engineering effective and creative software build real intellectual discipline — not just technical proficiency.

Their Code Conquest hackathon platform is designed to bring that philosophy into a competitive, community setting. With events spanning K–12 and divisions for Elementary, Jr. High, and High School, KidOYO has built a first-of-its-kind regional league right here on Long Island.

The event is livestreamed, judged by computing educators, and brings together teams from across Nassau and Suffolk counties. It's as close as high school gets to a professional hackathon.
